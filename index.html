Hi, I'm Noura!

My name is Noura Alami, and if I had to describe myself in one sentence, I would say I am someone who is endlessly curious about the world and the people in it. I was born and raised in Lebanon, a small country with one of the richest cultures, histories, and cuisines in the entire world. Growing up Lebanese shaped everything about who I am — the way I connect with people, the importance I place on family and community, the pride I feel in my heritage, and the deep appreciation I have for food, music, and storytelling. Lebanon is a place where ancient ruins sit next to modern cities, where you can ski in the mountains in the morning and swim in the Mediterranean in the afternoon. It is a country that has faced immense hardship but remains one of the most vibrant and resilient places I have ever known.

But my love for culture does not stop at my own. One of my greatest passions in life is traveling and discovering new places, new people, and new ways of living. I believe that experiencing other cultures is one of the most powerful things a person can do — it teaches empathy, broadens perspective, and reminds you that there are so many beautiful ways to exist in this world. Every country I visit leaves something with me: a recipe, a phrase, a memory, a new way of seeing things. I speak English, Arabic, and French, which has opened so many doors for me when traveling and connecting with people from different backgrounds. I am currently studying at Northeastern University in Boston, but no matter where I am, I carry Lebanon with me everywhere I go.
