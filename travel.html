My World: Culture & Travel

I am Lebanese, and my culture is not just where I come from — it is who I am. Lebanon is a country of contradictions and contrasts: ancient and modern, calm and chaotic, heartbreaking and breathtakingly beautiful. Growing up, I was surrounded by the warmth of Lebanese hospitality, the smell of our food, the sound of Arabic mixing with French in everyday conversation, and the deep sense of community that comes with being part of such a close-knit culture. These experiences gave me an appreciation for identity and belonging that I carry with me everywhere. But they also gave me a curiosity — if my own culture is this rich, what else is out there? That question is what drives me to travel.

Every place I visit teaches me something new. I am drawn to the way culture shows up in small, everyday moments: the way people greet each other, the local foods at a market, the architecture of a neighborhood, the music playing from a window. Travel has made me more open-minded, more adaptable, and more grateful. I hope to keep exploring for as long as I can, and to one day visit every continent.

Places I Have Visited

  • 🇱🇧 Lebanon — my home, always the first place I return to
  • 🇫🇷 France — fell in love with Paris and the south of France
  • 🇹🇷 Turkey — Istanbul is one of the most magical cities I have ever seen
  • 🇦🇪 UAE — Dubai is a city unlike anything else in the world
  • 🇺🇸 United States — currently living in Boston, exploring the East Coast
